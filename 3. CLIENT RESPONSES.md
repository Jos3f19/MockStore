## Scenario 1: Transactions Unable to be created - Authentication Failed Error 102

### Client Report:
> "Since yesterday, our transactions have not been able to be generated due to an error message appearing in the service: 'Authentication failed 102.' The client is a merchant of great importance to the company and wishes to cancel the service due to the issues encountered."

---

### Management and Approach (Internal Analysis)

**Error Identification:**
- **Error Code 102** means: "TranKey hash does not match (wrong or malformed TranKey)"
- This is an authentication issue where the security key being sent doesn't match what the system expects

**Root Cause Analysis (with some assumptions):**
The most common causes for this error are:
1. The Secret Key (secretKey) was recently changed in the PlacetoPay panel but the merchant's system is still using the old one
2. There was a recent update or change in the merchant's integration code that affected the authentication generation
3. The secretKey is being incorrectly copied (extra spaces, missing characters, etc.)
4. The encoding process for generating the tranKey is not working correctly

**Recommended Internal Actions:**
1. **Immediate:** Check in our admin panel if the merchant's credentials were recently modified or regenerated
2. **Verify:** Confirm if there were any changes on our platform that could have affected this client
3. **Coordinate:** If the issue is on the merchant's side, prepare to assist their technical team with the correct implementation
4. **Priority:** Given the client's importance and desire to cancel, escalate this case for priority resolution

## Scenario 3: Merchant Confusion - Difficulty Understanding Service Implementation

### Client Report:
> "You are working on a project with the merchant 'Sunshine.' Despite having explained the service functionality and the processes required to implement the service multiple times and in different ways, the merchant continues to show confusion and difficulty understanding it. Although the merchant is not upset, they express frustration and suggest that you find new ways to explain the process, as they need a clear understanding in order to move forward with the project."

---

### Management and Approach (Internal Analysis)

- This is a **communication challenge**, not a technical issue
- The merchant is willing to learn but traditional explanations haven't worked
- They are frustrated but not angry - this is a positive sign of engagement
- The project is stalled until understanding is achieved

**Root Cause Analysis:**
Possible reasons for continued confusion:
1. **Learning style mismatch:** The explanations may be too technical, too abstract, or not aligned with how the merchant processes information
2. **Information overload:** Too much information presented at once without practical context
3. **Lack of visual aids:** Some people need diagrams, flowcharts, or videos to understand processes
4. **Missing real-world context:** The merchant may not see how the concepts apply to their specific business
5. **Terminology barrier:** Industry jargon may be creating unnecessary confusion

**Recommended Internal Actions:**
1. **Assess:** Identify the specific areas causing confusion - is it the entire process or specific parts?
2. **Adapt:** Prepare multiple formats of explanation (visual, written, video, hands-on)
3. **Simplify:** Break down the process into smaller, digestible steps
4. **Personalize:** Use examples specific to "Sunshine's" business model
5. **Collaborate:** Consider involving a colleague who might have a different communication style

---

---

**Root Cause Analysis:**

3. **Possible external pressures:** The merchant may be under pressure from their own stakeholders
4. **Expectation mismatch:** There may be unrealistic expectations about implementation timelines or complexity
5. **Possible personality conflict:** The merchant may respond better to a different team member

**Internal Actions:**
1. **Immediate escalation:** Inform my direct supervisor and account manager immediately
2. **Document everything:** Record all interactions, emails, and communications for protection and review
3. **Self-care:** I will not take personal attacks personally - this is about their frustration, not your competence
4. **Team involvement:** Consider bringing in a senior analyst or manager for the next interaction
5. **Account review:** Assess if this account is viable given the communication dynamics
6. **Set boundaries:** While we want to retain clients, I think abusive behavior should not be tolerated

**Other considerations**
- We can be empathetic and accommodating, but personal attacks and abuse are not acceptable
